# 📊 AtliQ Hardwares – SQL Business Insights Project

---
## 🎯 Problem Statement

AtliQ Hardwares lacks a structured and efficient way to answer ad-hoc business questions using SQL, leading to delays in decision-making and limited visibility into key business performance areas.

The business required a data-driven approach to quickly analyze sales, customers, products, and market trends to support faster and more accurate decision-making.

---

## 💡 Objective of the Project

This project was built to bridge the gap between raw data and business decisions by:

- Writing SQL queries to solve real business questions  
- Analyzing sales, product, customer, and channel data  
- Generating insights that help stakeholders make better decisions  

---

## 🏢 About the Company

AtliQ Hardwares is a global manufacturer of computer hardware products with a strong presence in:

- Asia Pacific  
- Europe  
- North America  
- Latin America  

### Business Segments:
- Personal Computing  
- Peripherals & Accessories  
- Networking & Storage  

---

## 🗃️ Dataset Overview

The analysis is based on a relational dataset containing:

### 📌 Dimension Tables
- `dim_customer` → Customer details, markets, channels  
- `dim_product` → Product information, segments, divisions  

### 📌 Fact Tables
- `fact_sales_monthly` → Monthly sales transactions  
- `fact_gross_price` → Product pricing data  
- `fact_manufacturing_cost` → Cost details  
- `fact_pre_invoice_deductions` → Discount information  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & SQL Skills Used

- SQL Joins (Inner Join, Left Join)  
- Common Table Expressions (CTEs)  
- Window Functions (Rank, Dense Rank)  
- Aggregations (SUM, COUNT, AVG)  
- CASE Statements  
- Date Functions  
- Group By & Sorting  
- Subqueries
